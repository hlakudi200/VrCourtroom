VR Courtroom, a pioneering virtual reality game transforming legal education.

VR Courtroom is an immersive, interactive platform that brings the complexities of legal practice into a virtual environment, allowing students to engage with real-life legal scenarios in a unique and engaging way. Developed in partnership between the University of Johannesburg’s Faculty of Law and the JBS Innovation Lab, this innovative tool leverages cutting-edge technologies such as virtual reality (VR) and artificial intelligence (AI) to create a transformative learning experience.

Bridging Theory and Practice in Legal Education

The VR Courtroom mission is to bridge the gap between theoretical legal knowledge and practical application. We aim to make legal education more accessible, engaging, and effective by immersing students in realistic courtroom simulations. By doing so, we prepare the next generation of legal professionals to tackle complex legal issues with confidence and competence.

The VR Courtroom Team

The development of VR Courtroom was a collaborative effort led by Prof. Michele van Eck, with technical leadership from Prof. Abejide Ade-Ibijola. The project also involved a dedicated team of undergraduate students from the JBS Innovation Lab, University of Johannesburg, who contributed to the game’s design and programming. Additionally, a group of legal experts from UJ’s Faculty of Law provided invaluable insights into the content and scenarios, ensuring the game’s relevance and accuracy in legal education.

Enter the Virtual Courtroom

Put on your VR headset and step into a lifelike courtroom environment. Explore the space, familiarize yourself with the courtroom layout, and interact with various elements.

Investigate and Prepare

Review case files, gather evidence, and interview virtual clients and witnesses. Use your legal knowledge to analyze the facts and build a strong case strategy.

Present Your Case

Take on the role of a lawyer and present your arguments before a virtual judge and jury. Engage in realistic court proceedings, including cross-examinations and objections, to advocate for your client.

Receive Feedback and Refine

After the trial, receive detailed feedback on your performance. Reflect on your strategy, learn from your mistakes, and replay scenarios to improve your legal skills.
